Introduction

This project analysis player data from the 2018 FIFA World Cup squads. The goal is to create involving, pre-tournament insights and infographics that highlight team and player characteristics, including age, experience, and positional breakdowns.

Scenario

I am working as a data analyst creating pre-tournament content for a sports analysis platform. My goal is to help fans and media understand team and player trends before the tournament begins.

Ask

Business Task: Explore and summarize trends among players in the 2018 FIFA World Cup squad to gain insights into:

  • What is the average age of players per team?
  • Which teams have the most experienced players?
  • How are players distributed across different position groups?
  • What patterns emerge when grouping players by age, position, and experience?

Process

# Clean column names and prepare data
squads <- squads %>%
  janitor::clean_names() %>%
  mutate(
    age = as.numeric(age),
    caps = as.numeric(caps),
    goals = as.numeric(goals),
    position_group = case_when(
      grepl("Goalkeeper", position, ignore.case = TRUE) ~ "Goalkeeper",
      grepl("Defender", position, ignore.case = TRUE) ~ "Defender",
      grepl("Midfielder", position, ignore.case = TRUE) ~ "Midfielder",
      grepl("Forward", position, ignore.case = TRUE) ~ "Forward",
      TRUE ~ "Other"
    )
  )

Analyze

# Summary statistics by team
team_age_exp <- squads %>%
  group_by(team) %>%
  summarize(
    avg_age = mean(age, na.rm = TRUE),
    total_caps = sum(caps, na.rm = TRUE),
    total_goals = sum(goals, na.rm = TRUE),
    .groups = "drop"
  )

# Position breakdown
position_counts <- squads %>%
  count(position_group, sort = TRUE)

Share

# Average Age by Team
ggplot(team_age_exp, aes(x = reorder(team, avg_age), y = avg_age)) +
  geom_col(fill = "#1f77b4") +

    title = "Average Age by National Team - 2018 FIFA World Cup",

# Total International Caps by Team

Key Insights:

  1. Teams with a higher average age (like Costa Rica and Mexico) can bring more experience, but may face physical challenges.
  2. Teams with many international matches (such as Spain and Germany) suggest extensive international experience that could influence performance.
  3. Balanced positional groups demonstrate team structure: some teams rely heavily on defenders, while others prefer midfield control.

Act

# Flag teams with highest and lowest average age
age_extremes <- team_age_exp %>%
  arrange(avg_age) %>%
  slice(c(1, n()))

# Identify teams with most experienced squads
top_experience <- team_age_exp %>%
  arrange(desc(total_caps)) %>%
  slice_head(n = 5)

list(
  "Youngest and Oldest Teams" = age_extremes,
  "Top 5 Most Experienced Teams" = top_experience
)

Recommendations

  1. Feature Veteran Teams: Highlight teams with the most experienced players as likely to perform well under pressure.
  2. Youth Spotlight: Promote younger teams as underdogs with high potential and energy.
  3. Balance Analysis: Examine how experience and age mix within top-performing squads.
  4. Position-Based Profiles: Use position group trends to create fan-focused content around roles (e.g., top midfielders by caps).
